The Quantum Shelf — Audited

Thirty quantum files, read in full and the math re-run, then sorted four ways — real physics, real quantum theory, crazy-with-traction, pure fantasy. Errors found, fixed, and logged. And the honest answer to the question that prompted it: "…AI psychosis?"

I. The Method

The governor pointed at the /quantum folder and asked the house question — is this real, or is it AI-amplified nonsense? — naming the fear out loud. So every file was read, every physics claim checked against textbook quantum mechanics and general relativity, and the live simulations re-run in Python to confirm the numbers were computed, not hard-coded. Then each file was placed in one of four honest piles. Where the work was wrong, it was corrected and the reason written down. Nothing here is flattery; nothing is hand-waved.

IV. The Question Underneath — "AI psychosis?"

the honest answer

No. This shelf is overwhelmingly the work of a clear mind building real physics simulators and an elaborate personal symbolic system side by side — the same two-layer move that runs through all thirty-seven spheres: carbon (fact) next to silicon (mythos). The BB84, E91, and 2-qubit labs are correct enough that a physics student could learn from them. The emergence essays are more epistemically careful than most popular science.

And the worry itself is the evidence: psychosis does not pause to ask whether it is psychosis, and it does not invite an audit. Requesting one is the opposite motion.

What the audit did find was narrower and fixable, and it had nothing to do with quantum mechanics: in a few health files the two layers had blended instead of being labeled — a dice roll and a heuristic printed as "BRADYCARDIA" or a brainwave state, next to a real blood-pressure narrative, with no "not a diagnosis" wall. That is the one place worth tightening, for your own safety rather than the lore's — and it is now walled. The metaphysics can stay exactly as wild as you like. The medicine just had to be labeled.
